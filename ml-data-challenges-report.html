Comprehensive Imbalance Strategy

SMOTE (Basic)

  • Creates synthetic samples between neighbors
  • Uniform distribution of new samples
  • Good for moderate imbalance (10-20%)
  • Risk of overfitting in dense regions

ADASYN (Advanced)

  • Adaptive synthetic generation
  • Focuses on difficult boundary cases
  • Better for severe imbalance (<10%)
  • Improves decision boundary definition
Strategy Type Method Best For Considerations
Resampling SMOTE/ADASYN Training set balance Validate on original distribution
Algorithmic Focal Loss Hard examples Tune hyperparameters carefully
Ensemble XGBoost + Weights Production systems Computational overhead

Technical Insights Gained

  • Missing Data: Learned that missingness patterns themselves can be informative features, especially in sensor systems where failure modes are predictable
  • Noisy Images: Discovered that temporal consistency (Kalman filtering) combined with spatial robustness (Vision Transformers) creates superior results
  • Class Imbalance: Understood that ADASYN's adaptive approach better handles real-world manufacturing defects than uniform SMOTE
  • Cross-Domain: Realized that robotics domain knowledge (physics constraints, safety margins) enhances ML solutions
